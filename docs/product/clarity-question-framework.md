# Clarity Question Framework

## Question 1

### What problem are we solving?

**Why this matters**

37% of projects fail because requirements are unclear.

Source  
Project Management Institute — *Pulse of the Profession: Strategy Implementation Report*  
https://www.pmi.org/learning/library/pulse-of-the-profession-2018-strategy-implementation-11821

**Suggestions**

- Teams ship features before clearly defining the real problem.
- AI tools generate ideas faster than teams can evaluate them.

---

## Question 2

### Who is experiencing this problem?

**Why this matters**

42% of startups fail because there is no market need.

Source  
CB Insights — *The Top Reasons Startups Fail*  
https://www.cbinsights.com/research/startup-failure-reasons-top/

**Suggestions**

- Early-stage founders struggle to define product scope before building.
- Designers using AI tools struggle to turn ideas into real products.

---

## Question 3

### What does success look like?

**Why this matters**

52% of projects experience scope creep due to unclear goals.

Source  
Project Management Institute — *Pulse of the Profession Report*  
https://www.pmi.org/learning/thought-leadership/pulse

**Suggestions**

- A clear sprint plan the team agrees on before development starts.
- A defined outcome that determines whether the sprint succeeded.

---

## Question 4

### What constraints should we consider?

**Why this matters**

Large IT projects run 45% over budget on average.

Source  
McKinsey & Company — *Delivering Large-Scale IT Projects on Time, on Budget, and on Value*  
https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-digital/our-insights/delivering-large-scale-it-projects-on-time-on-budget-and-on-value

**Suggestions**

- A small team with limited engineering time.
- AI tools generating ideas faster than teams can prioritise them.

---

## Question 5

### What is the smallest version we could build?

**Why this matters**

80% of product features are rarely or never used.

Source  
Standish Group — *CHAOS Report 2015*  
https://www.standishgroup.com/sample_research_files/CHAOSReport2015-Final.pdf

**Suggestions**

- A simple tool that helps founders define a sprint in minutes.
- A lightweight workflow that clarifies a project before building.

---

## Question 6

### What could make this project fail?

**Why this matters**

17% of IT projects fail so badly they threaten the company's survival.

Source  
McKinsey & Company — *Delivering Large-Scale IT Projects on Time, on Budget, and on Value*  
https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-digital/our-insights/delivering-large-scale-it-projects-on-time-on-budget-and-on-value

**Suggestions**

- Solving the founder’s problem instead of the customer’s problem.
- Starting development before the team agrees on the scope.
